Projects

My research examines how environmental constraints, institutions, and public policies shape production, land use, and development in low- and middle-income countries. It combines microeconometric methods with field surveys, administrative data, environmental data, and cross-country datasets.

ECOTERM: Ecosystem services of termite mounds in Cambodia

ECOTERM is an interdisciplinary research project on the ecosystem services provided by termite mounds in agricultural landscapes in Cambodia. It brings together economists, ecologists, anthropologists, botanists, and soil scientists.

Within the project, I lead the economics work package. Our work investigates how termite mounds affect rice production, farmers’ input use, land allocation, and rural livelihoods. We combine repeated farm and plot-level surveys with ecological information to understand whether termite mounds act as productive assets, biodiversity reservoirs, or constraints for agricultural households.

Current work includes research on termite mounds and rice yields, as well as a study of their implications for input allocation and agricultural misallocation.

Related work

Agriculture and rural environmental change in China

This research programme examines the sustainability of agricultural production in China, with a particular focus on family farming, input use, environmental performance, and the consequences of industrial development for rural areas.

My work has studied environmental efficiency in conventional and non-certified organic rice farming, social interactions and input spillovers among family farmers, and the effects of industrial water pollution on rice production. It shows that environmental performance depends not only on the production technology itself, but also on farmers’ experience, local interactions, and exposure to external environmental pressures.

Selected publications

  • Guo, H. and S. Marchand (2019). Social interactions and spillover effects in Chinese family farming. The Journal of Development Studies, 55(11), 2306–2328.

  • Marchand, S., M. Barro, H. Xiong, and H. Guo (2025). Industrial water pollution and farmer adaptation: Evidence from rice farming in Jiangsu, China. Agricultural Economics, 56(1), 108–123.

Media, air pollution, and environmental governance in China

This project studies the role of information and public attention in environmental regulation. It asks whether media coverage can act as an informal mechanism of accountability by influencing citizens, local governments, and firms.

Using daily data on media sentiment, air quality, weather, and industrial activity across Chinese cities, this research examines how the emotional tone of environmental news is associated with pollution outcomes. Current extensions focus on social media discussions and on firms’ emissions behaviour.

Selected publication

Forests, land use, and forest-based development

My research on forests builds on my doctoral work on institutions and deforestation in developing countries, as well as on my postdoctoral experience in Brazil.

In 2012, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the Federal University of Viçosa, working in collaboration with the Institute for Applied Economic Research (IPEA) in Rio de Janeiro. I worked with José Gustavo Feres as part of the Rede Amazônia Sustentável (RAS; Sustainable Amazon Network), an interdisciplinary initiative led by Toby Gardner on the social and environmental dimensions of land-use change in the Brazilian Amazon.

This line of research has addressed the relationship between agricultural productivity and deforestation, conservation incentives for local governments, the persistence of low-income and environmentally degrading land uses, and the effects of log export bans on wood processing and employment in developing countries.

Climate risks, vulnerability, and adaptation

A more recent part of my research examines how climate risks affect production systems and how public policies can support adaptation in developing countries.

This work includes research on the effects of extreme climate events on agricultural efficiency in Vietnam, the measurement of physical vulnerability to cyclonic risks, the valuation of insurance against low-probability risks, and the allocation of international adaptation finance.

Selected publications and chapters
