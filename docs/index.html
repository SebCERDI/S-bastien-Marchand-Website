Email Google Scholar ORCID HAL

Development · Environmental · Agricultural Economics

I am an Associate Professor (HDR) in Economics at CERDI, Université Clermont Auvergne.

My research lies at the intersection of environmental and development economics. I study how environmental constraints, institutions, and public policies shape production decisions and economic transformation in developing countries.

I combine microeconometric methods with farm, firm, household, and country-level data. I have worked on deforestation and land-use change in the Brazilian Amazon; agricultural production, pollution, and farmer behaviour in China and Cambodia; and trade and industrial policies in forest-based sectors.

My current projects examine the effects of termite mounds on rice production and agricultural input allocation in Cambodia, the role of media sentiment in environmental regulation in China, and whether national adaptation planning affects the allocation of international climate finance.

Before joining CERDI in 2013, I was a postdoctoral researcher in Brazil, based at the Federal University of Viçosa and working in collaboration with the Institute for Applied Economic Research (IPEA) in Rio de Janeiro. I worked with José Gustavo Feres as part of the Rede Amazônia Sustentável (RAS; Sustainable Amazon Network), an interdisciplinary research initiative on the social and environmental dimensions of land-use change in the Brazilian Amazon, led by Toby Gardner.

Selected work

Enhancing employment through log export ban: Insights from wood-processing firms in developing countries

Sébastien Marchand and Mouhamed Zerbo. Forest Policy and Economics (2025).

Using firm-level data across developing countries, this article studies how log export bans affect employment in wood-processing industries.

Mass media as an environmental watchdog? Evidence from the war on air pollution in China

Sébastien Marchand, Damien Cubizol, Elda Nasho Ah-Pine and Huanxiu Guo. Environment and Development Economics (2025).

This article examines whether the emotional tone of environmental news functions as an informal regulatory force during China’s campaign against air pollution.

The colonial origins of deforestation: an institutional analysis

Sébastien Marchand. Environment and Development Economics 21(3), 318–349 (2016).

A historical-institutional analysis of cross-country differences in deforestation across former colonies.
